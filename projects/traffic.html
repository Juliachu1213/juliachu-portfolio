1. Introduction

In recent years, the concept of reverse marketing has gained increasing popularity among enterprises. Unlike traditional marketing that persuades consumers to purchase products, reverse marketing focuses on attracting customers through the intrinsic characteristics and value of products. This study applies the idea of reverse marketing to the tourism industry in Yilan, Taiwan ‚Äî a region famous for its natural scenery yet suffering from strong seasonal fluctuations in tourist volume.

During holidays, heavy traffic on National Highway No. 5 leads to congestion, while off-peak seasons cause local businesses to face drastically reduced customer flow. To address this imbalance, this research predicts hourly visitor distributions at major tourist attractions by integrating traffic, weather, social media, and search engine data. The predictions support data-driven reverse marketing strategies that help businesses plan promotions and manage seasonal demand more effectively.

2. Prior Work

  • Reverse Marketing: Value-driven approaches (e.g., Shopee, Nike, Philippines Tourism Board) emphasize brand authenticity and user engagement.
  • Traffic Flow Prediction: Machine learning and deep learning models (Random Forest, SVR, LSTM, XGBoost) outperform traditional methods (ARIMA, Kalman Filter).
  • Social Media and Search Data: Instagram hashtags and Google Trends provide real-time signals for predicting tourism dynamics.

3. Methodology

The study analyzed data from January 2019 to December 2020 to forecast tourist flow from March to May 2021. The integrated dataset contained 8,784 records with 12 features and one target variable, combining traffic, weather, social media, and search data.

  • Traffic: Real-time highway data aggregated hourly.
  • Tourist Statistics: Visitor counts from 19 attractions, focusing on five key sites.
  • Weather: Hourly temperature, humidity, wind, and precipitation.
  • Social & Search: Instagram (Instaloader) + Google Trends (Pytrends).
  • Models: Random Forest, SVR, Linear Regression, LSTM, XGBoost (with MSE, RMSE, MAE, MAPE, R¬≤ metrics).

4. Results ‚Äî Example: National Center for Traditional Arts

Three non-temporal models and two temporal models were evaluated to predict hourly visitor flow. Random Forest achieved the best performance among non-temporal models, while XGBoost outperformed all models overall.

5. Prediction Results ‚Äî National Center for Traditional Arts (May 2021)

Using the XGBoost model, the predicted total number of visitors for May 2021 was approximately 44,102, representing a decrease of about 3,000 compared to April. The first two weekends of May (May 2 and May 9) showed the highest peaks, while traffic gradually declined throughout the rest of the month.

Visitor peaks occurred between 2 PM and 4 PM, while weekly averages remained steady at around 800 visitors per day. These predictive insights support reverse marketing strategies ‚Äî encouraging businesses to promote special offers or cultural events during off-peak periods to enhance visitor engagement and economic sustainability.
